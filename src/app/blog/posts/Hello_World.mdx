## Hello, World!

Hello there! I'm **Vedant Patel**, a Mechanical Engineering student at UNC Charlotte, and this is my little corner of the internet. You may have found this page by accident or actually meant to click on this link. Either way, welcome!

## So, who am I?

I'm a junior mechanical engineering major with a ton of classes like Engineering Mechanics and Advanced CAD on my plate. But let me tell you, I spend as much time outside of class coding, prototyping, and making things as I do in class.

My comfort zone is at the intersection of hardware and software. I love it when something physical I made actually works — be it a rocket launch that goes off without a hitch or a sensor that reads out exactly what it should.

## What have I been up to?

A few things I'm proud of:

- I'm a member of the **Charlotte Rocketry Club**, where we design and assemble real launch systems. It's exactly as cool as it sounds.
- I'm also part of **AIAA (American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics)** — great people, great conversations about aerospace.
- As an **Autodesk Design and Make Ambassador**, I get to mentor other students and creators on design and manufacturing, which I genuinely love doing.

One of my favorite recent projects was building a **Water Analyzer and Biofilter System** — I designed and 3D-printed the enclosure in Fusion 360, programmed a microcontroller with turbidity and TDS sensors, and built a biofilter to test water quality in remote areas. It was messy, fun, and I learned a ton.

## What do I work with?

On the technical side, I'm comfortable with Python, C++, Java, AutoCAD, MATLAB, and a bunch of other tools. I've got certifications in AutoCAD and Python, and I've gotten pretty familiar with hardware like Raspberry Pi along the way.

## What's this blog for?

Honestly? I want a place to share what I'm learning, what I'm building, and the occasional thought that feels worth writing down. No pressure, no algorithm to please — just me documenting the journey.
