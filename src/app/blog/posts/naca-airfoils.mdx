## Recreating NACA Airfoils

For the last few weeks, I have been recreating NACA airfoils, which are the mathematically determined wing profiles that became the standard for modern aerodynamics, at least back in the 1940s. It begins with entering the NACA equations into a coordinate generator, importing hundreds of points into CAD, and working magic to produce those beautiful, iconic shapes. Each airfoil has its own character, and getting the geometry right is actually rather satisfying.

## Now It's Time to Build

Of course, I now have the itch to actually make the thing. I intend to use FDM 3D printing first, which is quick, cheap, and perfect for iterating. However, I also have my sights set on resin printing for the surface finish that FDM just cannot achieve, which, for an airfoil, actually does matter.

The idea of taking an 80-year-old aerospace formula and holding the physical manifestation of that formula in my hand is rather thrilling.
