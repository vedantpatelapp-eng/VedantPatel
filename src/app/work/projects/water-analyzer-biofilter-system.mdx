## Overview

This project was developed as part of ENGR 1300 at UNC Charlotte. The goal was to design and build a fully functional water quality analyzer capable of operating in remote areas without access to laboratory infrastructure. The system combines a custom 3D-printed enclosure, embedded sensor programming, and a handbuilt biofilter to deliver real-time water quality data in the field.

## Key Features

- **3D-Printed Enclosure**: Designed and printed the full system housing in Fusion 360, optimizing the layout for sensor placement, structural integrity, and ease of assembly.
- **Sensor Integration**: Programmed a microcontroller to interface with turbidity and TDS (Total Dissolved Solids) sensors, enabling real-time water quality readings.
- **Biofilter System**: Built and tested a biofilter stage that improves water clarity prior to analysis, reducing interference and improving measurement accuracy.
- **Team Collaboration**: Worked closely with teammates to refine the mechanical layout, sensor placement, and overall system performance through iterative testing.

## Technologies Used

- **Fusion 360**: For designing and modeling the full system enclosure prior to 3D printing.
- **Microcontroller (Embedded C/Python)**: For programming sensor logic and real-time data output.
- **Turbidity & TDS Sensors**: For measuring water clarity and dissolved solids concentration.
- **3D Printing**: For rapid prototyping and fabrication of the system enclosure.

## Challenges and Learnings

One of the primary challenges was calibrating the turbidity and TDS sensors to produce consistent readings across varying water conditions. Fine-tuning the biofilter to adequately pre-treat water before it reached the sensors required multiple iterations of testing and redesign. This project reinforced the importance of tight feedback loops between mechanical design and embedded systems when building integrated hardware solutions.

## Outcome

The final system successfully demonstrated real-time water quality analysis in a compact, field-ready form factor. The project sharpened skills across CAD, embedded programming, and systems integration, and highlighted the potential for low-cost hardware solutions to address real-world access-to-clean-water challenges.
